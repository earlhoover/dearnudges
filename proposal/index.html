Joining Dear

A proposal for Giacomo, in writing.

The shape of it

Before any number, the philosophy. Dear is bootstrapped. There is no investor, no exit plan, no liquidity event scheduled. The way you share in what we build is through phantom equity: a written contractual right to a cut of what Dear earns or sells for, paid in cash when those triggers fire, vesting over time.

It is not real ownership of Elsewhere Index LLC. There are no shares, no votes, no K-1, no tax obligations on you before money has flowed. Just a written promise that scales with the company. If Dear works, this is real. If Dear doesn't, this costs you nothing.

Two cuts, layered.

Cut A · when Dear is sold

You receive 5% of net proceeds from any sale of Dear or Elsewhere Index LLC. Net means sale price minus broker fees, legal costs, and any debt outstanding. If Dear is never sold, this is zero.

Cut B · ongoing share of monthly revenue

You receive 3% of Dear's monthly net revenue above $10,000 MRR, paid monthly. Net means revenue after Apple's platform cut and infrastructure costs. Below $10K MRR, this is zero.

Vesting

Both Cut A and Cut B vest on the same schedule. Four years, with a twelve-month cliff.

The cliff is the first 12 months. During the cliff, nothing vests. If you leave inside it, you forfeit everything. Once the cliff crosses, 25% of your full grant becomes yours, immediately. From there, another 1/48th vests each month until you are fully vested at month 48.

What active engagement means

Vesting requires active engagement. We define this loosely. You are considered active if you contribute at least one substantive deliverable per month, or maintain ownership of one shared artifact (the marketing dashboard, the Instagram content calendar, weekly memo, similar). We do not track hours.

If you go silent for a full quarter, vesting pauses for that quarter and resumes when you re-engage. Mutual: this is your protection too. If Dear shuts down or stops operating, your vested portion stays vested.

What this is not

  • Not real ownership of Elsewhere Index LLC. You hold no membership units.
  • Not voting rights or board representation.
  • Not a K-1. Income from Dear, when it flows, comes through your portage employment, not as partnership distribution.
  • Not an obligation. If Dear never makes meaningful money, you owe nothing back.
  • Not a salary. While you are part-time and unpaid, the phantom equity vests but pays nothing. Cash flows only when triggers fire.
